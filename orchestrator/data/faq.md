## Visit basics

### Where is the farm?

SAAC FARM, 1 Elliot Road, Singapore 458686. The gate is on Elliot Road; look
for the blue SAAC sign. Free street parking is available along Elliot Road
and the side road opposite the entrance.

### What are the opening hours?

Daily, 09:00–17:00 SGT (Asia/Singapore). Last tour starts at 17:00 and runs
for the standard slot duration; see `booking_rules.yaml` for the exact
slot length and per-day overrides.

### How long is a tour?

The default slot is 60 minutes. School groups occasionally book the longer
90-minute slot — confirm with the farm before quoting it.

### How many people per slot?

Up to 30 pax per slot. Larger groups must split across two slots or arrange
a private booking.

### Is the farm wheelchair-accessible?

Most pathways are flat compacted gravel; the main barn and wash station are
step-free. Let us know in advance if anyone in your group uses a wheelchair
so we can route you via the side gate.

## Bookings

### How do I book a tour?

Send us a WhatsApp message with your preferred date, time, and group size.
We will confirm available slots and ask for a deposit to lock the booking.

### Can I edit or cancel a booking?

Yes — reply to the WhatsApp thread. Edit and cancel both require a plain
`YES` to confirm. Cancellations inside the deposit window may forfeit the
deposit; see your confirmation message for the exact deadline.

### Do you take a deposit?

Yes. A SGD 50 deposit is required to confirm a booking, payable by PayNow
at least 48 hours before the slot. The UEN is sent with the deposit request.

### What payment methods do you accept?

PayNow only for the deposit. On-the-day extras (e.g. farm produce) are paid
by cash or PayNow on site.

## Tours

### What does a standard tour include?

A guided walk through the vegetable beds, the chicken coop, the goat pen,
and the wash station. Children get a short feeding demo (lettuce leaves only,
supplied by us). The tour ends at the farm shop with cold drinks available
for purchase.

### Are animals safe for small children?

Yes — the goats and chickens are calm and used to visitors. Children must
stay with a guardian at all times. We do not allow children to enter the
goat pen unsupervised.

### Can we feed the animals?

Yes — we provide lettuce leaves for the goats and chickens. Outside feed is
not allowed (citrus, bread, and meat are harmful to the animals).

### Can we picnic on the farm?

Yes, after your tour, at the picnic tables near the farm shop. Outside food
is welcome; please take your rubbish home.

## Practical

### What should I wear?

Closed-toe shoes are required (the paths are gravel and there are goats).
A hat and sunscreen are recommended. Bring a refillable water bottle — we
have a cold-water tap at the wash station.

### What happens if it rains?

Tours run in light rain — we have covered areas for the animal segments.
For thunderstorms we will reschedule at no charge or refund your deposit.

### Are pets allowed?

No, with the exception of registered service animals. Please notify us in
advance if you are bringing a service animal.

### Can I take photos?

Yes, personal-use photography is welcome. Commercial shoots and drone
flights require written permission — contact us via WhatsApp to arrange.
